Project Overview

Oneseventech is a web-development agency based in NYC and works mainly with upcoming startups.

The goal was to rebrand 17WebDev as Oneseventech in order to stand out within the oversaturated NYC startup ecosystem.

In April, 2020 - a 3x client of mine - 17WebDev reached out to rebrand itself as Oneseventech. They wanted to attract more startups, and they wanted to do it by 31st May, 2020: Memorial day in the US, 17webdev’s 3rd anniversary and just barely a month away.

This was a long project - so I'll stick to the best bits and tell you how & why I did them, even as Covid-19 hit hard and disrupted everything.

Debunking Assumptions

Time was of the essence, but good research never fails.

Discovery phase

We had initially assumed that most of the successful founders would be in their late-20s, so our idea of the brand was something that would attract a young demographic.

The data clearly suggested we had to change gears.

While the brand identity designer and digital marketer defined the brand, I worked closely with the founder coming up with concepts for the landing page.

Expereimenting with elements
  • The "highlighter" effect was very popular,
  • but subtle made more sense.

Later, we'd combine these "concepts" to create a landing page.

The “soft launch”

Just as we were starting to feel confident about the brand, Covid-19 hit and the lockdown spree made everything twice as hard.

With comms down, there was no way to launch the brand by 31st May, but it was too perfect of a day to ignore.

Filler website Figma design

I pitched the idea of creating a filler website: a one-page website that would showcase the services and the latest project but also make it clear that a better version was on the way.

Webflow - almost 100% resemblance.

In the next two days, I worked with Figma and Webflow to design, develop and, make it responsive.

We had survived.

Talk like a user

Almost every competitor on the market had sites with tons of distraction - as if they had robots for customers instead of actual humans.

We decided to take a more humanised approach, and make the design conversational.

Conversation starter.

Not only did it give Oneseventech an edge, it also got more attention than the 8 seconds I’m (apparently) entitled to.

  • Using social proof...
  • ...to capture leads.

The team loved the conversational bits and I continued using them to tie sections of the webpage together, creating a flow that feels like a journey.

Way more intuitive.

I also decided to remove client names and logos:

Polarising? Yes.

Good decision? Also yes.

A person looking to hire OneSeven is far more likely to identify with “AR Furniture App” or “published in Bloomberg” over the name of the client that nobody knows.

Backup brigade

The case study

The case studies were a big factor in showing competency & reliability.

I used the case studies as an opportunity to showcase more of Oneseventech's process - expanding over the homepage section of the same name.

Publications and Our Process.
  • Projects stats,

  • Downloadable, just in case.

There are several other less-impactful ones, such as reusing the NYC skyline for the About page, putting in clever copy like “Preferably our next client” when you click “who you are” etc. that contributed to the overall experience.

Impact

The research helped Oneseventech take more decisions based on data rather than public opinion, thus reducing time from conception to execution.

An established style guide helped developers spin up websites quickly, irrespective of platform.

On the other hand, a set of flexible rules allowed marketers to create external assets such as emails, templates, social media etc.

A 12% increase (on average) in engagement when posted with new design on social channels.

Project Learnings

  • User research saves lives.

    It saved the business from making a decision that probably would have tanked it.

  • Humans over robots

    Anything that doesn't overburden and oversell to the user is a good strategy.

  • Knowing code doesn’t hurt.

    Lots of debate on this, but coding is one of the handiest skills going forward.

At the time of writing this, I’m still in touch with the team going over small iterations and ideations over the final design before pushing it into development.
